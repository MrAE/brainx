NSF Global Brain Data Coordination Conference

Where: Johns Hopkins University, Baltimore, MD USA

When: Thursday April 7-8, 2016

Host: NSF, Kavli Foundation, & Kavli Neuroscience Discovery Institute

FAQ

Have a question that isn't addressed below? Email us at erika.lance@jhu.edu

Who will be in attendance?

Representatives from all of the large national and international neuroscience intiatives, including the US BRAIN, the EU Human Brain Project, Japan Brain/MINDS, Korea Brain Project, China Brain Science Project, Brain Canada, Taiwan, and Australia.

In addition, representatives from NSF, Kavli, Allen Institute for Brain Science, and HHMI will be present.

Why is this meeting happening?

The purpose is twofold. First, two coordinate ideas and lessons learned internationally for all issues surrounding data, including management & analysis. Second, to provide NSF with a set of recommendations for which aspects of data are most important to fund in the coming fiscal years.

When will it be held?

The conference will take place over 1.5 days, Thursday April 7 & 8, 2016. Thursday will be a full day of conference, friday we will end at noon to let people get home to be with their families for the weekend.

Where will it be held?

The conference will be held at Johns Hopkins University, hosted by the brand new Kavli Neuroscience Discovery Institute. Attendees from out of town will be staying across the street at the Inn at the Colonade.
